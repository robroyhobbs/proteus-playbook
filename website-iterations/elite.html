Credibility is table stakes. The decision is about competitive advantage at the margins. Peer-driven, not sales-driven.

The buyer's world

You work with pro athletes or elite programs. Your reputation is your business. You don't adopt technology because it's trendy — you adopt it because it gives you an edge your competitors don't have. You've seen every "revolutionary" tool. Most end up in the corner.

What would make you look? Peer validation from trainers you respect. Specific data on how it changes programming. Evidence athletes want to use it.

Messaging architecture

Primary: "Data you can't get elsewhere"

No other system measures 3D force production across all movement planes. Defensible uniqueness claim.

Secondary: "Competitive advantage at the margin"

At elite level, small differences matter. The trainer who sees what others can't programs better.

Objection: "One more thing?"

Must integrate into existing workflows, not require rebuilding the system. Show practical daily use.

Tone: Peer-to-peer

This audience resists being "sold to." Content should feel like one practitioner sharing findings with another. Technical language welcome.

Where they discover

  • Peer network: Stroupe, Cressey, Walsh mentioning Proteus in their content — most powerful channel
  • LinkedIn: Practitioner-to-practitioner, not brand content
  • Conferences: NSCA, CSCS, private performance summits
  • YouTube: "Stroupe's Proteus Assessment Protocol" — deep, technical

Lead capture

  • "Advanced 3D Protocols" — real protocols from Stroupe, Cressey, Walsh
  • Practitioner Network: Private community — peer discussion, protocol sharing
  • Quarterly roundtable: Invite-only sessions with top Proteus trainers

4-email nurture (peer proof)

  • 1: Protocol guide + "What Stroupe looks for in rotational assessment"
  • 2: Cressey video walkthrough of real assessment
  • 3: "How [trainer] added Proteus to assessment day without changing their system"
  • 4: Peer intro — "Want to talk to someone who's used it for 2 years?"

Conversion: Relationship-driven

  • Elite trainers don't fill out forms. Path: content → community → peer conversation
  • Ambassadors = sales force. Certified Trainers convert peers.
  • Best CTA: "Come see it at Stroupe's facility"

Key insight: You're not optimizing for volume. You're optimizing for the right 50-100 people who influence the next 500.
